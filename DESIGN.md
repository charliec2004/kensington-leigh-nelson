# Kensington Leigh Nelson Portfolio Design

## Direction

The site should feel simple, stylish, minimal, professional, and clean. It should present Kensington as an emerging finance professional with a polished academic and internship profile:

- Chapman University graduate
- Business major
- Economics minor with an emphasis in finance
- Intern at IEQ Capital in San Francisco
- Interested in wealth management

The visual tone should be tasteful and composed, closer to a refined professional profile than a loud personal brand site.

## Visual Principles

- Keep the page elegant and restrained. Use whitespace, typography, and proportion as the main design tools.
- Make the portrait feel intentional, prominent, and polished. It should be larger and more forward in the hero while still supporting the professional story.
- Favor a quiet editorial or private-wealth tone over generic startup gradients, oversized cards, or decorative clutter.
- Use a small number of recurring visual motifs so the page feels unified from top to bottom.
- Make every section feel connected to the previous one through shared spacing, rules, typography, and motion patterns.
- Use the "new graduate profile" framing as the content posture: clear education, internship experience, and career direction, presented with confidence and restraint.
- Give the portrait a professional backing, such as offset squares, layered panels, or aligned blocks that echo the grid/rule system elsewhere on the page.

## Motion And Interaction Principles

- Animations must be tasteful, subtle, and purposeful. Motion should clarify hierarchy, guide attention, or make transitions feel smoother.
- Avoid animations that feel flashy, random, distracting, or disconnected from the content.
- Use continuity between elements: a line, accent, portrait frame, section marker, or text reveal should feel like it belongs to the same system throughout the page.
- Interactions should feel considered. Hover states, focus states, section reveals, and button motion should use consistent timing and easing.
- Prefer a few well-designed motion moments over many small effects.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences. The site must remain polished and complete when animations are disabled.
- Preserve readability during motion. Text should never slide, fade, or transform in a way that makes it hard to read.

## Interaction Details To Carry Into Build

- Navigation links should have a refined hover treatment, such as a subtle underline or rule movement.
- Primary actions should have restrained tactile feedback: a small lift, rule shift, or background change.
- Section entrances should feel continuous, not isolated. For example, content can reveal upward in a consistent rhythm as the reader scrolls.
- The portrait area can use a soft reveal or mask transition, but it should remain professional and understated.
- The portrait backing should participate in the interaction system. Offset squares or panels can subtly parallax, reveal, or align on load, but the motion must be slow, precise, and calm.
- Repeated elements should share the same interaction language so the site feels intentionally designed rather than assembled.

## Accessibility And Professional Standards

- Maintain strong color contrast and clear focus states.
- Keep motion short and calm, with no essential information hidden behind animation.
- Avoid hidden side effects and surprise interactions.
- Use explicit, readable copy. The site should sound confident and clear, not overly clever.
